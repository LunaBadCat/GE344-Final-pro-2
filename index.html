Conflagration

is a large fire. Conflagrations often damage human life, animal life, health, and/or property.
A conflagration can begin accidentally or be intentionally created.
A very large fire can produce a firestorm, in which the central column of rising heated air
induces strong inward winds, which supply oxygen to the fire. Conflagrations can cause casualties
including deaths or injuries from burns, trauma due to collapse of structures and attempts
to escape, and smoke inhalation.

Causes and types
